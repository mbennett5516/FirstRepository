A long time ago, in a galaxy far far away...

I went to High School and explored a lot of different things that I may want to do with my life. I was a trombone player in band, I was in drama, sang in my church choir, and excelled academically...

None of that mattered. I still didn't know what I wanted to do with my life.

Then a few years after graduating I decided to try something new and enrolled in DeVry University as a Game and Simulation Programming student to learn how to make video games for a living. Unfortunately, DeVry ended the program without allowing my class to graduate, leaving us with heavy debt and no marketable skills or a degree.

A few years after re-joining the workforce doing just odd-jobs here and there, I decided that debt or no debt, I should go back into programming. What I did learn, I thouroughly enjoyed, so I looked for a place that would be affordable, and would teach me what I needed to know in order to make a living. I found the opportunity with SMU and TEK systems, and the rest, as they say, is history.
